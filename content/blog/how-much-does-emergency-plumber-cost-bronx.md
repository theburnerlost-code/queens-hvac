If you've ever called an HVAC technician at 2am and gotten a number that made your eyes water, you know how confusing HVAC service pricing can be. This guide breaks down what Queens homeowners actually pay for the most common HVAC service emergencies — no fluff.

## The Short Answer

Emergency HVAC service in Queens typically runs **$150–$500 for simple repairs** and **$500–$2,500+ for major work** like refrigerant line repair or HVAC unit replacement. After-hours surcharges from most companies add $50–$150 to any job. We charge the same rate 24/7.

## Pricing by Job Type

### AC Breakdown Repair: $180–$750
- Accessible pipe (under sink, near shutoff): $180–$350
- Pipe inside a wall (requires drywall access): $350–$750
- Main water line: $500–$1,800

### Duct Cleaning: $120–$400
- Simple sink or tub clog: $120–$200
- Hydro-jetting (deep clean): $250–$400
- Main refrigerant line clearing: $300–$600

### HVAC Unit Repair: $150–$450
- Pilot light or thermocouple replacement: $150–$250
- Element replacement (electric): $200–$350
- Full replacement (40-gal gas, installed): $850–$1,200

### Thermostat Repair: $95–$350
- Flapper/fill valve replacement: $95–$180
- Full thermostat replacement (installed): $250–$500

## What Affects the Price

**Access difficulty** is the biggest variable. A pipe inside a wall costs 2–3x more than the same pipe under a sink. **Permit requirements** add cost for major work like main line replacement. **Parts availability** — if we need a part we don't have on the truck, that adds time and cost.

## How to Avoid Getting Overcharged

1. Get the price in writing before any work starts
2. Ask if the quote includes parts and labor
3. Ask if there's an after-hours surcharge
4. Confirm the HVAC technician is NYC licensed (check the license number)
